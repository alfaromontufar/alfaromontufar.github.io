Carlos A. Alfaro Banco de Mexico

I am researcher at Banco de Mexico (also known as BANXICO, that is the Central Bank of Mexico), where my principal activities consist in developing and implementing optimization models. I received my Ph.D. degree in 2014 from the Department of Mathematics at CINVESTAV under supervision of Carlos E. Valencia. Between 2011 and 2012, I visited the HP Labs at Palo Alto, CA to work with Burcu Aydin, Kemal Guler, Lyle Ramshaw, Pano Santos and Bob Tarjan. There, I developed algorithms to study tree-structure data, and supported the implementation of assignment algorithms for resource planning allocation.
